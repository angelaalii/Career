As of 2011, an estimated 123,160 garbagemen were employed in the United States. During that year, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that trash collectors made an average of $16.55 an hour and earned an average of $34,420 a year. The median-earning 50 percent of garbage collectors in the United States reportedly received hourly wages of between $11.45 and $20.52 and annual incomes of between $23,820 and $42,680.Nearly half of all garbage collectors worked for private waste collection services in 2011 and made an average of $33,380 a year. Those who worked for waste treatment and disposal plants averaged $37,080 a year. More than 40 percent of garbage collectors were employed and paid by the government. The majority worked for local governments and made an average of $35,590 a year, while the few employed by state governments earned an average annual income of $40,080.
